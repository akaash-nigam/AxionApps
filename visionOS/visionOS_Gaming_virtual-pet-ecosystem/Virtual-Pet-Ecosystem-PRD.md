# Virtual Pet Ecosystem - Product Requirements Document
*Spatial Gaming in the AI Era*

---

## Section 1: Game Concept & Abstract

Virtual Pet Ecosystem creates truly persistent, intelligent creatures that live in your actual space and develop genuine emotional bonds with their human families. Unlike traditional virtual pets confined to screens, these magical companions explore your home, develop personalities based on your environment, form memories tied to specific locations, and continue "living" even when the app is closed. This revolutionary spatial pet experience transforms any home into a living ecosystem where digital creatures become authentic family members.

### Core Elements
* **Genre & Setting**: Life simulation and pet care with persistent spatial creatures
* **Unique Spatial Hook**: Pets exist as permanent residents in your physical space with true environmental intelligence
* **AI Integration**: Personality evolution, environmental learning, social dynamics, generational traits
* **Target Platform**: Utilizes Vision Pro's spatial persistence, environmental understanding, and emotional presence

**P1 - Important (Launch)**

**P2 - Enhanced (Post-Launch)**
- Advanced breeding with complex genetic systems
- Seasonal events and special occasion behaviors

**P3 - Future (Year 2+)**
- Cross-platform pet companions on multiple devices
- Real-world pet product integration and tie-ins
- Virtual pet marketplace and breeding exchanges

- Minimum play area: Entire accessible home space
- Interaction range: Natural reach for petting and feeding (1m radius)

- Frame rate target: 60 FPS with smooth creature animation
- Persistence requirement: 24/7 pet simulation even when app closed

## Appendix A: Spatial UI/UX Guidelines

### Comfort Zones
```
Near Field: 0.3m - 1m (direct pet interaction and care activities)
Mid Field: 1m - 3m (pet observation and household integration)
Far Field: 3m+ (pet territory awareness and environmental context)
```

### UI Placement Rules
- Pet status information follows pets naturally without blocking interaction
- Care tools accessible within natural reach during pet interaction
- Social notifications positioned to not interfere with pet bonding
- Emergency controls available from any location in pet's territory

## Appendix B: Gesture & Interaction Library

### Core Gestures
```
Petting: Gentle stroking motions across pet's body
Feeding: Cupped hand offering food items
Playing: Throwing motions and toy interaction
Calling: Open palm gesture with name vocalization
Affection: Two-handed embrace or gentle head stroking
```

### Care Gestures
```
Grooming: Brushing motions for pet hygiene
Training: Pointing and reward gestures for behavior
Health Check: Gentle examination movements
Breeding: Two-pet introduction and pairing gestures
Comfort: Soothing motions during pet distress
```
